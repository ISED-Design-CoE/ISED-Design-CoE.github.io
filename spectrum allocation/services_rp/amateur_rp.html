Amateur service: rules and policies 

The amateur radio service refers to the use of radio apparatus for the purpose of self-training, intercommunication or technical investigation by individuals who are interested in radio technique solely with a personal aim and without interest relating to money.    

In order to operate a radio in the amateur service, individuals require an amateur radio operator certificate.

Frequency bands

The Canadian Table of Frequency Allocations (CTFA) establishes the frequency allocations available for radio spectrum services in Canada. The spectrum allocation tool allows you to look up CTFA frequencies by service. From the tool’s Service(s) drop-down menu, select any or all of the services defined in the next section to determine which bands can be used for those purposes. 

You can also refer to the schedules published in RBR-4 — Standards for the Operation of Radio Stations in the Amateur Radio Service, which describe the frequencies allocated to operations in the amateur service. 

The Spectrum Outlook outlines ISED’s planning activities related to Canada’s radio spectrum over a five-year period, including anticipated changes to frequency bands and services. 

Definitions

The amateur radio service includes the services defined in the following table. 

Amateur service  A radiocommunication service for the purpose of self-training, intercommunication and technical investigations carried out by amateurs, that is, by duly authorized persons interested in radio technique solely with a personal aim and without pecuniary interest.  
Amateur-satellite service  A radiocommunication service using space stations on earth satellites for the same purpose as those of the amateur service. 

Certification

To learn how to obtain an operator certificate for amateur radio, refer to the amateur radio operator certification page.
