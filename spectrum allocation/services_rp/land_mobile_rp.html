Land mobile radio service: rules and policies  

The land mobile service is a radiocommunication service that provides communications between base stations and land mobile stations, or between land mobile stations such as mobile vehicles and portable radios (e.g. for public safety, farm operations, taxis and private networks).

Frequency bands

The Canadian Table of Frequency Allocations (CTFA) establishes the frequency allocations available for radio spectrum services in Canada. The spectrum allocation tool allows you to look up CTFA frequencies by service. From the tool’s Service(s) drop-down menu, select any or all of the services defined in the next section to determine which bands can be used for those purposes. 

The Spectrum Outlook outlines ISED’s planning activities related to Canada’s radio spectrum over a five-year period, including anticipated changes to frequency bands and services. 

Definitions

The following definition applies to the land mobile radio service. 

Land mobile service  A mobile service between base stations and land mobile stations or between land mobile stations. 

Licensing

For information on land mobile radio licences, refer to the land mobile radio service page. 

If you already hold a licence, look up your conditions of licence.
