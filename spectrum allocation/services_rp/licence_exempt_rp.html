Licence-exempt spectrum: rules and policies 

Licence-exempt spectrum refers to specific bands that are designated for unlicensed, low-power applications. This spectrum allows consumer devices to operate without a specific radio licence, as long as they comply with Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada’s (ISED) policies, regulations and technical standards. Examples of such consumer devices include baby monitors, Family Radio Service devices, RLAN technologies (e.g. Wi-Fi and Bluetooth), as well as dynamic spectrum access technologies. 

Licence-exempt spectrum may also be used for private networks.

Frequency bands

The frequency ranges designated for licence-exempt applications are specified in the technical documents listed in the previous section. 

The Spectrum Outlook outlines ISED’s planning activities related to Canada’s radio spectrum over a five-year period, including anticipated changes to frequency bands and services. 

Certification

Before wireless equipment can be imported, distributed or offered for sale in Canada, it must be certified.  

For information on certifying licence-exempt equipment, refer to the wireless equipment certification page. 

Certification applicants or manufacturers can use the services of one of ISED’s recognized Conformity Assessment Bodies, who will guide the applicants through the certification process. 

To become a recognized conformity assessment body (testing lab or certification body) you may access the following procedures, REC-CB and REC-LAB.
