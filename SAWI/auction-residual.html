Residual auctions process

The process for residual auctions by sealed bid generally includes the following steps:

1. Publication of notice/decision

2. Deadline for the receipt of applications

Submission period when prospective participants file their completed application forms with required documentation, including beneficial ownership information and financial deposits. The specific application requirements are detailed in the auction's consultation documents and the published licensing framework.

3. Publication of the list of applicants, beneficial ownership and affiliate associate information

Shortly after the application submission deadline, ISED publishes a comprehensive list of all applicants on its website. This disclosure includes information about beneficial ownership and affiliations between applicants, but does not indicate qualification status for auction participation.

4. Publication of the list of qualified bidders

ISED publishes the official list of qualified bidders on its website, specifying the licenses each bidder is eligible to bid on and their initial eligibility points for the auction.

5. Start of auction (sealed bid deadline)

6. Conclusion of auction

7. Publication of provisional licence winners

Official announcement of auction results, including disclosure of winning bidders, licenses won, and final prices, with a summary of the auction process.

8. Initial payment deadline

Date by which winners must submit their first payment installment, typically a percentage of the total winning bid amount. Failure to meet this deadline may result in forfeiture of winning bids.

9. Final payment deadline

Due date for the remaining balance of winning bids, which may occur in multiple stages for high-value auctions and must be completed before licenses can be issued.

10. Issuance of licences

Formal granting of licenses to winning bidders who have fulfilled all payment obligations. This marks the conclusion of the auction process.
